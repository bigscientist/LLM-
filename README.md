# LLM for novel with history and superheroines
This dataset contains the complete text of several novel, fully generated with the assistance of open-source large language models (LLMs). It represents an experiment in large-scale literary creation under a human-AI collaborative paradigm: the human author designed the story architecture, character settings, historical research, and narrative pacing, while open-source LLMs carried out text expansion, dialogue generation, scene depiction, and the weaving of multiple narrative threads based on detailed prompts.

The defining feature of this creative experiment is its superhero-inspired reimagining of historical figures. The real-life stories of Chinese revolutionary heroes—such as Zhu Feng (Ji Hongjuan), Wu Xianqing, Zhao Yiman (Li Kuntai), Chen Xiuliang, and Xiao Minghua—are interwoven with a sweeping eighty-year timeline, comic-book-style surreal elements (the "Project Rainbow" electromagnetic experiments, biological immortality, a masked vigilante lineage), and contemporary achievements in China's aerospace industry (the Long March rocket series, the Mars sample-return mission, the DF-17 and DF-61 strategic missiles). The result is a narrative world that is both deeply rooted in historical fact and brimming with romantic imagination. Fictional characters (such as Minister of State Security Chen Jing, aerospace engineer Chai Song, and mathematics prodigy Ji Wei) interact in the same timeline with real-world figures (such as Jensen Huang, Lisa Su, Yan Ning, and Meng Wanzhou), blurring the line between fiction and reality.

Chen Jing is an entirely fictional character. She was not designed to be a model public servant, but rather a figure of internal contradiction—someone who rides a shared bike to work, argues with netizens on Weibo, and is cursed to the top of trending searches yet never closes her comment section. She represents a fragile possibility: independent thought persisting within a vast institutional system.

In the AIGC-assisted creative process, the author first established Chen Jing's core tensions (rule of law vs. institutional inertia, procedural justice vs. outcome orientation), key experiences (from Supreme Court judge to Deputy Minister of State Security, driving anti-espionage legal reforms), and signature behaviors (posting on Weibo, replying to netizens, riding shared bikes). The LLM then generated her dialogue, internal monologues, official speeches, and online interactions for each chapter based on these parameters. Her controversial remark about Xiao Minghua—"If you cover up the name, it just looks like a love-struck fool"—and the subsequent wave of public backlash that followed, were both generated by the model through multiple iterations, autonomously producing responses consistent with her established personality. It was not the human author writing every word; it was the model "understanding" this person's behavioral logic and reacting accordingly.

This is the core experimental value of AIGC narrative: given a sufficiently complex character core, let the model deduce her words and actions in specific situations. The reason Chen Jing leaves such a strong impression is, in a sense, because she is genuinely "alive"—every speech she delivers, every silence she keeps, every Weibo draft she writes and deletes, is a natural extension of that core within the model.

The dataset includes both the complete prompts used in each round of dialogue and the corresponding model-generated outputs, enabling researchers to reproduce and compare results.We hope this dataset will provide valuable material for research and creation in several directions: the challenges of long-form narrative coherence with LLM assistance, the ethical boundaries of historical fiction, and the possibilities of merging the "superhero" genre with modern Chinese history. Whether you are an NLP researcher, a creative writing practitioner, or simply a reader curious about this century-spanning tale, you are welcome to freely use, analyze, and reimagine the contents of this dataset.

# AIGC文学创作数据集-历史英雄与超级英雄
本数据集收录了完全由开源大语言模型（LLM）辅助生成的几部小说的完整文本。这是一次以“人机协同”模式进行大规模文学创作的实验：人类作者负责故事架构、人物设定、历史考据和叙事节奏把控，开源LLM负责根据详细提示词进行文本扩写、对话生成、场景描写和多线叙事的衔接。

创作的核心特色在于“历史人物的超级英雄化演绎”——将朱枫、吴先清、赵一曼（李坤泰）、陈修良、萧明华等真实隐蔽战线英烈的事迹，与跨越八十年的时空跨度、漫画式的超现实设定（如“彩虹计划”电磁实验、永生体质、蒙面女侠传承）以及当代中国航天成就（长征系列火箭、火星采样返回、DF-17与DF-61战略导弹）交织在一起，构建出一个既根植于真实历史、又充满浪漫主义想象的故事世界。小说中的虚构人物（如国安部长陈静、航天工程师柴忪、数学天才及薇）与真实历史人物（如黄仁勋、苏姿丰、颜宁、孟晚舟等）在同一时空中互动，模糊了虚构与现实的边界。

陈静这个人物完全出自虚构。她的设计初衷，不是要塑造一个“完美的人民公仆”，而是要塑造一个在体制内部推动改革的、充满矛盾的人——骑共享单车上下班、在微博上和网友互怼、被全网骂上热搜却从不关闭评论区。她代表的，是一种在庞大系统中保持独立思考的、脆弱的可能性。

在AIGC辅助创作过程中，作者首先为陈静设定了核心矛盾（法治信仰vs.体制惯性、程序正义vs.结果导向）、关键经历（从最高法法官调任国安部副部长、推动反间谍法治化改革）和标志性行为（发微博、回复网友、骑共享单车）。然后由LLM根据这些设定，在每一章的具体场景中生成她的对话、心理活动、发言稿和网络互动。她关于萧明华的那句“如果把名字盖住，就是恋爱脑”，以及后来被全网围攻的回应，都是在多次迭代中由模型根据她的性格设定自主生成的——不是人类作者逐字写好，而是模型“理解”了这个人的行为逻辑之后，给出的符合她性格的反应。

这就是AIGC叙事的核心实验价值：给定一个足够复杂的人物内核，让模型去推演她在具体情境中的言行。陈静之所以令人印象深刻，某种程度上是因为她真的是“活”的——她的每一次发言、每一次沉默、每一次删了又写的微博草稿，都是这个内核在模型中的自然延伸。

本数据集同时包含了每一轮对话中使用的完整提示词与模型对应的生成结果，以供研究者复现和对比。我们希望通过这个数据集，为以下方向的研究与创作提供素材：大模型辅助长篇叙事的连贯性挑战、历史虚构文学的伦理边界、以及“超级英雄”类型与中国近现代史融合的可能性。无论您是NLP研究者、创意写作实践者，还是单纯对这段跨越百年的故事感到好奇的读者，都欢迎自由使用、分析和再创作本数据集中的内容。我们希望通过这个数据集，为以下方向的研究与创作提供素材：大模型辅助长篇叙事的连贯性挑战、历史虚构文学的伦理边界、以及“超级英雄”类型与中国近现代史融合的可能性。无论您是NLP研究者、创意写作实践者，还是单纯对这段跨越百年的故事感到好奇的读者，都欢迎自由使用、分析和再创作本数据集中的内容。
